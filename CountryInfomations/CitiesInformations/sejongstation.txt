[Sejong Station]

Sejong Station is a South Korean research facility located in Antarctica. 
It serves as the country's permanent research station on King George Island, part of the South Shetland Islands. 
Named after King Sejong the Great of Korea, the station was established in 1988 and is operated by the Korea Polar Research Institute (KOPRI). 
Sejong Station is involved in various scientific research activities, including studies on climate, biology, and atmospheric conditions. 
The station plays a crucial role in advancing South Korea's contributions to Antarctic research and international scientific collaboration.
